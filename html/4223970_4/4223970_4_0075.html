The Rev. Krawczunas selected a committee of old women and of the so-called male singers who examined the parish books. As a matter cf course, the committee vindicated the priest, printed its report and distributed it among the people.

We are publishing the report in full text.

"The committee's report of the examination of the books of St. George's Parish, Chicago, Illinois.

We, the undersigned committee, have been elected by the public at a parish meeting, at the church of St. George, Bridgeport, Chicago, Illinois, the 7th day of August, this year, to examine the parish books and the budget from the date of establishment of this church to the present time. We are assuring our parishioners that the parish books and the budget were kept in the best order by Rev. Krawczunas, and only by the efforts and elevation of the priest was it possible to keep the books in such good order.

2

"The income and expenses of every year were stated plainly in separate items, and the balance of each year stated how much money was left, how much money was short.

"Only in the year 1896 was it stated by mistake that there were $350.88 short, and on account of this minor mistake it looked as if in that year no money was left in the budget. But after correction of that mistake, we find $223.50 in the budget, and the shortage was only $127.32

"We found that the Polish nuns were brought to the church by the rector, and that for the last four months of 1897, the wages to the nuns were paid by Rev. Krawczunas out of his own pocket.

"The fourteen lots, church and the residence deeded in the name of St. George parish and the Catholic bishop of Chicago.

What do you brothers think after you have read this report? What do you say for such soaping of your eyes? The question is for what does the Rev. Krawczunas with his selected committee hold his parishioners? Fools? Does it look to them as if there is no one in the parish who knows how to read. In this so-called committee report is not one word of truth, it is a lie from the beginning to the end.

The committee in its report assures the parishioners that they examined the books from the inception of the parish up to the present time, and that they found the Rev. Krawczunas' statements were made with accuracy and efficiency; that the yearly general income and expenses show clearly how much surplus and how much shortage of the money there was, and that after the accounting, the committee found, up to every cent, that the Rev. Krawczunas’ report was done in good faith.

If the priest's committee had found a correct statement of income and expenses and while the committee showed to the parishioners only the expenses, why then did the committee conceal the income. Were their brains unable to find them or did the priest asked them to conceal it? The committee 5 boasted that all the income and expenses were correctly found, but in its own report stated that the Rev. Krawczunas expenses were $41,758.50, and for the benefit to the parish, the priest assumed the debt of $19,077.48. Is this the account of Rev. Krawczunas' management of the parish during the past five years? Where is the income? Did the parishioners give nothing during those past years? Did Rev. Krawczunas pay from his own pocket? Where is the truth?

The committee in its report states that $11,200 were paid for the lots. We are asking the committee, who paid that $11,200 for those lots? Did Rev. Krawczunas pay for them? When our Rev. Krawczunas was living in the old country, the lots at that time were almost paid for. When Rev. Krawczunas came to Chicago, he found that on the parish lots there was only $600 of debt. But, when Rev. Krawczunas bought two more lots for $3,600 the priest paid only $4,200. but not $11,200, which his committee showed to us and wants us to believe. Where is the truth? Maybe the committee, by way of vindicating itself, will say that this report is not only that of Rev. Krawczunas, but the report of Rev. Kolesinskis shows the same. The committee willingly showed the expenses of both priests, but made no effort 6 to show the income of Rev. Krawczunas, nor of Rev. Kolesinskis.

Why is the committee trying to blind the contributing parishioners? Why are you holding the people up as laughing stock? Where is the money that the Rev. Krawczunas himself stated in both of his statements? Did the money sink into the pocket of Rev. Krawczunas, or was the money spent for the reception of the Polish guests, or was the money lost in some other place?

Let us take once more a glimpse into the statement of Rev. Krawczunas himself, as given to the parish: In the statement of 1895, Rev. Krawczunas states

Where has the balance of $36,981.28 vanished? Did the priest swallow it?

Let us divide the balance of $36,981.23 for the last three years of Rev. Krawczunas' income. It would be over $34 per day. Besides this profit mentioned, the priest had his income from mass, baptism, nuptial ceremonies and funerals; also the priest was traveling in other small towns where the Lithuanians are living, collected money from them, and yet even this profit was not enough. For this reason our priest was forced to take $34 per day from the parish treasury for his own personal needs, while at the same time the priest agreed to fulfill all the parish duties for the salary of $70 per 8 month. Really, our little priest is very gluttonous. If in Chicago we would have five such gluttonous men, they would eat up all our wages, we could not even be able to make enough for them, and we ourselves would be forced to live without any eating.

We are standing on the facts given by the priest himself in the year 1896. Where is the account of 1897 and 1898? Did the parishioners give nothing for the parish during the last two years, because the priest shows no income? Did the priest obtain an order from God to go to Lithuanian homes twice a year and extort money from every one as much as he could and to keep for himself the extorted money? When anybody asks the priest to give account of the money he denounces him as infidel, but when you are keeping quiet then everything is all right. What name, then, can we give to the priest's committee and the priest himself?

The committee in its report says: "Rev. Krawczunas kept the account of the parish with great care. Of course, if Rev. Krawczunas was able to eat up $34 per day, including all other profits, that shows how much "effort" he had put to it. We see his "effort," that without such "effort," 9 he could not have been able to consume so much money per day.

The committee was trying to prove that the priest had brought the nuns himself, and that during the four months' time the priest fed and paid them wages from his own pocket. We are asking the committee, who demanded those nuns? The parish? The priest demanded them because he was uneasy without the nuns. If the nuns are serving the priest, wash his laundry, then let the priest pay them. but not the parish. If the nuns are serving the priest in the same way as his girl servant, is it necessary then, for the parish to pay the wages for the priest's servants?

The selected committee boasted in its report that the committee for inspection of the parish books was elected at the public meeting at St. George's church. This is falsehood: About this parish meeting the public knew nothing; the meeting was called by Rev. Krawczunas himself, not by the church. When almost all the people left the church, and while in the church remained only a few old women and a few of the so-called singers. 10 the priest held the meeting with those ignoramuses in the name of the parish. And then they elected such a committee, who wrote the report, not according to the statement in the book, but according to the priest's order, in order to deceive the parishioners. During the last three years Rev. Krawczunas held no parish meeting; the priest feels that the public meeting will be dangerous for him; he is as afraid of one as the Jew of holy water, and the priest is trying his best to cast dust into the parishioners' eyes.

If this meeting had been held with the parish’s consent, the societies would make no protest. Today, the societies of the Province of God, Saint John the Baptist and Saint George, protested, and we believe other societies will make protest and will seek justice. The parish ought to get rid of such treasurers who are eating as much as $34 per day. If, for the last three years, the debt of $19,000 was made, then there is no question that for the next three years another such debt will be made. If we keep such a treasurer, the debt will never be paid. We must protect ourselves from 11 debts; every day we are sinking into an abyss.

The priest must be a priest, his duty is to watch the altar and perform religious service, but not to do business with the hard-earned money of the people, or to hide the money somewhere.

All the societies must agree among themselves to call a general meeting, and at such a meeting elect a committee, and then this committee should go to the bishop and present to him the condition of the treasury of our parish. If the spiritual government would not sweep out such a cashier, then we will go to the civil government, because the civil government's duty is to protect the public from exploiters. Not the priest alone is guilty for wasting the parish money, but the priest's committee is guilty too, for soaping the public's eyes, and for covering the priest's faulty conduct.
